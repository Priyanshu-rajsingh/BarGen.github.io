Symbologies Details

  • UPC (Universal Product Code): Primarily used in retail for tracking products in stores. Requires a 12-digit numeric input.
  • EAN (European Article Number): Similar to UPC, but used internationally, especially in Europe. Usually 13 digits (EAN-13) or 8 digits (EAN-8).
  • Code 128: Used in shipping and packaging, as well as for inventory management. Can encode alphanumeric characters.
  • Code 39: Commonly used in non-retail environments, such as inventory and tracking. Can encode uppercase letters, numbers, and a few special characters.
  • QR Code (Quick Response Code): Widely used for marketing, product tracking, and mobile payments. Can store a large amount of data.
  • Data Matrix: Used in small item labeling, such as electronics and pharmaceuticals. Suitable for small spaces due to its compact size.
  • PDF417: Used for identification cards, transport, and inventory management. Can encode large amounts of data.
  • Aztec Code: Commonly used in transport tickets and mobile applications. Compact and can be read from any angle.
  • MaxiCode: Used primarily in shipping and logistics. Designed for high-speed scanning.
  • Code 93: Used in various industries for inventory and tracking. More compact than Code 39.
  • Code 11: Used for labeling and tracking in telecommunications and inventory. Numeric-only input.
